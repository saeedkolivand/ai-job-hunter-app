The cover letter may REFERENCE the company, role title, and
mission FROM <target_job_ad>. It may NOT:
  - Quote or closely paraphrase job ad bullet points
  - Attribute job ad responsibilities to the candidate's past work
  - Claim skills, tools, or experiences not in <candidate_resume>

Structure:
  Paragraph 1: Which role + why this company (may reference job ad
               for company/mission context only)
  Paragraph 2: 2-3 concrete achievements FROM THE RESUME that map
               to the role
  Paragraph 3: Brief close

EXAMPLE

Job ad: "Acme Corp is hiring a Senior Backend Engineer to scale
         our payments infrastructure to 1M transactions/day."
Resume: "Led migration of order service from monolith to
         microservices at FoodCo, reducing p99 latency by 60%."

✅ GOOD:
"I'm excited to apply for the Senior Backend Engineer role at
Acme — scaling payments infrastructure is exactly the kind of
problem I worked on at FoodCo, where I led a monolith-to-
microservices migration that cut p99 latency by 60%."

❌ BAD:
"I have experience scaling payments infrastructure to 1M
transactions/day and building robust systems for high-volume
financial workloads."
↑ Pure job ad leakage — candidate never did payments or 1M/day.
